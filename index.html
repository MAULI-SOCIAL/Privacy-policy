Privacy Policy

Effective Date: April 10, 2026

This Privacy Policy governs the use of the “MAULI” platform operated by Western Estate, designed to facilitate transparent, efficient, and accountable communication between citizens and their representatives and administrators.

1. About the Platform

MAULI is a digital grievance redressal platform operated by Western Estate to enable citizens to submit and track public work issues within the constituency for rapid implimentation and up to date information on FAQs

2. Information We Collect

  • Full Name
  • Mobile Number
  • Location of area of affected (if provided)
  • Grievance details, requests, or feedback submitted
  • Interaction data (timestamps, status updates, etc.)

3. Purpose of Data Collection

  • Registering and processing citizen grievances
  • Forwarding requests to relevant departments or representatives
  • Providing updates and communication
  • Generating anonymized insights for administrative planning
  • Improving public service delivery

4. Data Usage & Governance

Data is used strictly for governance, administrative, and public service improvement purposes. Authorized personnel may access data for resolution and monitoring. Aggregated data may be used for dashboards and insights.

5. Data Sharing

We do not sell personal data.

Data may be shared:

  • With government departments for grievance resolution
  • When required by law
  • For internal administrative purposes

6. Data Security

We implement reasonable security measures to protect user data. However, no system is 100% secure.

7. Data Retention

Data is retained only as long as necessary for grievance resolution, administrative purposes, and legal compliance.

8. User Rights

  • Request correction of data
  • Request deletion (subject to legal/admin requirements)
  • Contact us for concerns

9. Third-Party Services

The platform may use third-party services such as messaging APIs and cloud infrastructure necessary for operation.
